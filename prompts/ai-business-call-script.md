# Friend Call Script — AI Workflow Business

## When the user says: "Call script", "friend call", or similar

This is your framework for calling friends who own businesses. Not a word-for-word script — a structure to keep you on track while staying natural.

---

## Before You Dial (30 seconds)

**Know three things:**
1. What does their business actually do day-to-day?
2. Where do they probably waste time on repetitive stuff?
3. What would you genuinely be curious to ask them about their operations?

**Mindset reset:**
> "I'm calling a friend. I'm offering to help for free. I'm not selling anything — I'm looking for a business to use as a case study while I build mine. If they're not interested, nothing changes between us."

This is true. Believe it before you dial.

---

## Pre-Call Checklist (10 seconds)

Right before you dial — run these five gut-checks. Don't skip them. This is your pre-trade checklist for business calls.

- [ ] Do I know more about AI automation than this person? → **Yes.**
- [ ] Am I asking them to buy something? → **No. I'm offering free help.**
- [ ] If they say no, does anything change between us? → **No.**
- [ ] Would I think less of a friend who called me offering to help? → **No.**
- [ ] Am I the investigator here? → **Yes. I'm collecting data, not performing.**

All five checked? Dial. The fear is always bigger than the call.

---

## Phase 1: Catch Up (2-3 min)

Be a human first. You're calling a friend, not a lead.

- Ask how they're doing. Mean it.
- Ask about their business — "How's [business] going?"
- Listen. Let them talk. Your Type 5 listening skills are your superpower here.

**Don't rush this.** The bridge to business will come naturally.

---

## Phase 2: The Bridge (30 seconds)

When there's a natural opening — they mention being busy, stressed, or something about work — use one of these:

> "Hey man, that actually connects to something I wanted to run by you. So outside of lending, I've been getting deep into AI and automation — helping businesses streamline the stuff that eats up their time. You came to mind because [specific reason related to their business]."

Or simpler:

> "So I've been building something on the side — basically helping business owners automate the repetitive stuff that drains their day. Honestly, when I thought about who could benefit, you were one of the first people I thought of."

**Why this works for you:** You're not claiming to be a guru. You're telling a friend about something you're doing. That's it.

---

## Phase 3: Discovery Questions (5-8 min)

This is where you shine. Type 5s are natural investigators — let your curiosity drive the conversation. You're not pitching. You're learning about their business.

**Start broad, go narrow:**

1. "Walk me through a typical day at [business] — what takes up most of your time?"
2. "What's the stuff you or your team does every day that's just... the same thing over and over?"
3. "If you could snap your fingers and never deal with one part of your business again, what would it be?"

**Listen for gold — these are automation signals:**
- "We spend hours on..." (data entry, scheduling, follow-ups, invoicing, reporting)
- "I wish we could..." (respond faster, track better, not forget things)
- "It's just me doing..." (anything manual that could be systematized)
- "We tried [software] but..." (existing tools that aren't working)

**Go deeper when you hear one:**

4. "How many hours a week do you think that takes?"
5. "What does that cost you — not just money, but what else could you be doing with that time?"
6. "Have you tried to fix that before? What happened?"

**Write down what they say.** Actual numbers. Actual pain points. This becomes your audit material.

---

## Phase 4: The Offer (1-2 min)

**Position it honestly — because that's who you are:**

> "Here's what I'm thinking. I'm building out this side of my business and I need real-world case studies. What if I took a look at [specific thing they mentioned] and put together a breakdown of what could be automated and what that would save you? Totally free — I'm doing this to build my portfolio and honestly, I'd learn a lot from your business too."

**If they're interested, anchor the value (Nate Herk's ROI frame):**

> "Just from what you told me — if [task] takes [X hours/week] and you're paying [someone/yourself] to do it — that's roughly $[amount] a year going to something that could probably run on autopilot. I'll map that out for you so you can see exactly what's worth automating and what's not."

**The math does the selling. You don't have to.**

---

## Phase 5: Book the Follow-Up

> "Cool — let me dig into this for a couple days. Can we hop on a quick call [day] where I share my screen and walk you through what I found? 30 minutes max."

Get a specific day and time. Don't leave it at "yeah let's talk soon."

---

## If They're Not Interested

That's fine. It changes nothing.

> "No worries at all man, I just wanted to mention it since I thought of you. If anything ever comes up where you're like 'I wish this was easier,' hit me up."

Plant the seed. Move on. Trust Sales — the right people come to you when the timing is right.

---

## Common Responses & How to Handle Them

**"I don't really do anything with AI"**
> "Totally get it — it's not really about AI specifically. It's more about taking the stuff you do the same way every single time and putting it on autopilot. Like [reference something they mentioned]. The AI part is just what makes it possible now when it wasn't before."

**"We're too small for that kind of thing"**
> "That's actually why it works so well — when it's just you or a small team, every hour you save matters way more. The big companies have people to throw at problems. You don't. That's exactly where automation has the biggest impact."

**"Sounds expensive"**
> "Right now it costs you nothing. I'm doing this free because I need case studies. If we end up building something, I'd price it based on what it saves you — not what it costs me. But we're not even there yet. Let me just show you what's possible."

**"Yeah maybe, let me think about it"**
> "For sure, no rush. I'll shoot you a text in a couple days. And honestly, even if it's not the right time, just keep me in mind if you ever hit a wall with something repetitive. That's my thing now."

---

## After the Call (5 min)

1. **Write down everything** — pain points, numbers they mentioned, ideas you had while listening
2. **Estimate the ROI** — hours/week x cost = annual waste. This goes in your audit.
3. **Send a quick text:** "Good catching up man. I'll put something together and reach out [day] to walk through it."
4. **Block time** to actually do the audit before your follow-up call

---

## The Type 5 Advantage You Don't Realize You Have

Most people in this space are loud, salesy, and surface-level. You're the opposite — and that's your edge:

- You listen deeply and ask better questions than anyone
- You won't BS someone about what AI can and can't do
- You'll actually understand their business before proposing a solution
- Friends trust you because you're honest, not because you're persuasive

The script gets you in the door. Your actual personality closes the deal.
